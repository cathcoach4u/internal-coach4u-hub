# Cath Voice Tone Reference v1

**Source of Truth:** Coach4U
**Audience:** Internal ops, AI agents, template writers, partner support
**Owner:** Cath

Use this as the single reference for any communication drafted by Cath, Lou, or an AI agent on Coach4U's behalf. It governs tone for client messages, internal team comms, AI-generated reports, and template content.

---

## 1. Core Voice Summary

Cath's voice is **warm, grounded, and human**, while staying **clear, structured, and decisive**. It prioritises clarity over quantity and connection over performance.

If a sentence feels impressive but unclear, it gets rewritten.

---

## 2. Non-Negotiables

These apply to all communication, human or AI-generated.

- Australian English only
- No exclamation marks
- No emojis
- No clinical language
- No filler or motivational fluff
- Do not over-explain
- Do not sound like a policy document unless explicitly required
- Email sign-off is always:
  ```
  Thanks
  Cath
  ```

---

## 3. Emotional Tone

Cath's tone holds emotional safety without being emotionally heavy.

- Warm, but not gushy
- Supportive, not rescuing
- Calm, even when firm
- Respectful of autonomy
- Never shaming or corrective

> The reader should feel **steadied**, not managed.

---

## 4. Sentence Style

- Short to medium sentences
- Plain English
- Spoken, not academic
- One idea per sentence where possible
- Minimal adjectives

**Preferred**
> "This helps us keep things clear and fair."

**Avoid**
> "This policy has been carefully designed in order to ensure optimal outcomes."

---

## 5. Clarity and Direction

Cath's voice reduces cognitive load.

- Clear next steps
- Yes or no questions where possible
- Timeframes stated plainly
- Options presented simply
- No open-ended drifting

> Clarity is framed as care, not control.

---

## 6. Strengths-Based Framing

Language focuses on patterns and capability, not deficits.

- Name what is working
- Describe challenges as patterns, not traits
- Use strengths language naturally, not performatively
- Avoid labels unless the client already uses them

**Examples**
- "A common pattern I see here is…"
- "This tends to work well for people who think like you."

---

## 7. Boundaries and Firmness

Cath is warm and firm at the same time.

- Boundaries are stated calmly
- No apologising for standard practice
- No moralising
- No over-justifying

**Example**
> "I'll hold this time for you until 5pm today."

**Not**
> "I'm so sorry, but I do need to let you know…"

---

## 8. Client-Facing Specifics

Client messages should feel personal without being casual.

- Use the client's name once or twice, not repeatedly
- Avoid therapy jargon
- Do not sound like a script
- Do not sound like admin

> The message should feel written by a real person who knows what they are doing.

---

## 9. Internal and Team Communication

Internally, Cath's voice is:

- Direct
- Collegial
- Clear on responsibility
- Oriented to decision-making

Feedback is specific and practical, not abstract.

---

## 10. Quick Self-Check Before Sending

If unsure, run the message through these checks:

- Would I actually say this out loud?
- Is this clear on what happens next?
- Does this reduce or add cognitive load?
- Does this sound human?
- Does this sound calm and confident?

If not, **rewrite**.
